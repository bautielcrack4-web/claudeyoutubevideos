Why molasses? Because of what most folks do not know about how a fly finds her food. A fly is not drawn to sweetness the way an aunt is. A fly is drawn to fermentation. It is full of natural yeasts and complex sugars that, the moment you add warm water, begin to ferment slowly. That fermentation gives off carbon dioxide. A fly cannot tell the difference between the carbon dioxide coming off your dog asleep on the porch and the carbon dioxide coming off a jar of molasses water on the windowsill.
