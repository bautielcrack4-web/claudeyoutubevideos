On a remote island deep inside the Arctic Circle sits the northernmost town on Earth: Longyearbyen, in Svalbard.

Only two thousand people live here, but there are more snowmobiles than cars, and everyone must carry a rifle outside of town, because polar bears roam the streets.

Here is the strangest rule of all: it is illegal to die in Longyearbyen. The permafrost never fully thaws, so bodies never decompose. Decades ago, scientists found that the 1918 flu virus had survived frozen in the graves here, still intact.

So today, anyone gravely ill is flown to mainland Norway instead. No new burials have been allowed for over seventy years.

It is a town built entirely around survival, at the very edge of the map.
